Course Description

This course examines the construction of gender and sexuality through Roman literature and archaeology. We will aim both to understand the Romans' conceptualization of concepts like love, masculinity, femininity, homoeroticism, courtship, and marriage, among others, and to use those investigations to understand our own in the modern day.

Course Objectives

  • to define and demarcate the boundaries of “sex,” “gender,” and “sexuality”
  • to articulate how the Romans defined, performed, and policed gender and sexuality
  • to compare Roman constructions of gender and sexuality with our own modern notions
  • to develop Latin (sight) reading skills across various genres and time periods
  • to become proficient at identifying scholarly arguments and evaluating the stages and evidence of argumentation
